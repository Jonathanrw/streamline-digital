# Personal Brand OS - User Guide

Welcome to the Personal Brand OS. This system is designed to turn your online presence into a streamlined authority business. Follow this guide to get started.

## 1. Setting Up Your Second Brain (PKM)
Your PKM is the engine of your brand.
- **Capture Everything:** Whenever you see a tweet, article, or video that sparks an idea, add it to the `Idea Inbox`.
- **The Weekly Review:** Every Sunday, go through your `Inbox`. Assign a `Topic` to each note and move the status to `Processed`.
- **Link to Content:** When drafting a post in the Content Engine, use the "Relation" field to pull in research from your PKM.

## 2. Launching Your Bio-Link Hub
Ditch the generic link-in-bio tools for a professional landing page.
- **Replace the Avatar:** Upload a high-quality, professional headshot.
- **Link Your Lead Magnet:** Ensure your "Free Success Checklist" (or current lead magnet) is at the top.
- **Showcase Your Best Work:** Use the `Products` gallery to feature your templates or consulting services.

## 3. Mastering the Content Engine
Consistency is built through systems, not willpower.
- **Idea Stage:** Dump all potential headlines into the `Content Calendar` under the `Idea` status.
- **Batching:** Once a week, move 3-5 ideas into `Draft`. Write them all in one sitting.
- **Scheduling:** Move drafts to `Scheduled` once you've set them up in your social media scheduler (e.g., Buffer, Hypefury).

## 4. Scaling with the Sponsorship Tracker
Don't wait for brands to find you.
- **Identify 10 Brands:** Add 10 brands you love to the `Sponsorship CRM`.
- **Find the Contact:** Research the marketing or brand manager and add their info.
- **The Pitch:** Move cards to `Pitching` as you send out emails. Track your conversion rate from pitch to `Signed`.

## Pro Tip: The Daily Standup
Spend the first 10 minutes of your workday in the Personal Brand OS Dashboard. Review your `Inbox`, check your `Scheduled` content for the day, and follow up on one `Sponsorship` lead.
