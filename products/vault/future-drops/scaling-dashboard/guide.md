# Strategy Drop #5: The Scaling Dashboard

## Overview
What gets measured gets managed. As a solopreneur, you need to transition from "tracking chores" to "tracking growth." The Scaling Dashboard provides the high-level metrics you need to make strategic decisions about where to invest your time and money.

## Key Metrics for Solopreneurs

### 1. The Core Four
*   **Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR):** For subscription products.
*   **Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC):** How much you spend on ads/tools to get one customer.
*   **Conversion Rate:** Percentage of landing page visitors who buy.
*   **LTV (Lifetime Value):** Average total revenue per customer.

### 2. The Efficiency Metric
*   **Revenue per Hour:** Total Monthly Revenue / Total Hours Worked. This helps you identify when it's time to raise prices or outsource.

### 3. The Growth Engine
*   **Email List Velocity:** New subscribers per week.
*   **Unsubscribe Rate:** Quality check on your content.

## Notion Template Specs: The Scaling Dashboard

### 1. Metrics Tracker (Monthly)
*   **Properties:** Month, Total Revenue, Total Expenses, Hours Worked, New Leads, New Customers.
*   **Formulas:**
    *   `Profit = Revenue - Expenses`
    *   `Hourly Rate = Revenue / Hours`
    *   `Conversion % = (Customers / Leads) * 100`

### 2. Quarterly Goals Database (OKRs)
*   **Properties:** Objective (e.g., "Launch The Vault"), Key Result (e.g., "100 Subscribers"), Deadline, Progress Bar (Rollup from sub-tasks).

### 3. Visual Dashboard Layout
*   **Top Row:** Call-out blocks showing current month's "Profit" and "Hourly Rate".
*   **Middle Row:** Linked view of OKRs (Gallery view).
*   **Bottom Row:** Chart (via Notion Chart or external tool like Rows/Snip) showing Revenue vs. Expenses over the last 6 months.
