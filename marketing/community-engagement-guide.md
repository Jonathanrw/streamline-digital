# Community Engagement Guide - Solopreneur Outreach

To build authority and drive organic traffic, we will participate in the following 3 communities. The goal is to provide value first, and only share our products when relevant.

## 1. Indie Hackers (IndieHackers.com)
*   **Target Group:** "Solo Founders" and "Notion Enthusiasts".
*   **Strategy:** Share transparent "Build in Public" updates. Specifically, post about the ROI of our content engine (e.g., "How I automated my content distribution in Notion").
*   **Value Add:** Answer questions about workflow optimization and scaling a one-person business.

## 2. Reddit (r/notion and r/solopreneur)
*   **Target Group:** Productivity seekers and solo business owners.
*   **Strategy:** Provide detailed "Show & Tell" posts of specific Hub modules (like the CRM or Finance tracker). Use screenshots to show utility.
*   **Value Add:** Offer free templates or the "Success Checklist" when people ask for help organizing their business.

## 3. Product Hunt Discussions
*   **Target Group:** Early adopters and tool-builders.
*   **Strategy:** Engage in "Productivity" and "No-Code" discussions. Share insights from our blog posts about automation.
*   **Value Add:** Give feedback on other people's products and build relationships with other creators for future affiliate partnerships.

## Rules of Engagement
1.  **Be Helpful First:** No spamming links. Only share a link if it directly solves a problem mentioned in a comment.
2.  **Be Transparent:** Always disclose that you are part of the Streamline Digital team.
3.  **Track Referral Traffic:** Use UTM parameters where possible to track which communities are driving the most conversions.
